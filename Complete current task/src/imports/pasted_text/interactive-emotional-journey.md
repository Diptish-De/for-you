I actually think there's a much better direction.

The current design is **beautiful**, but it's still a **scrolling website**. After the first minute, it becomes passive—you just scroll and read.

What happened to her today wasn't passive. Someone shattered her trust. If you want this to leave a lasting impression, make it feel like **she is interacting with someone who genuinely cares**, not consuming a webpage.

I'd turn it into an **interactive journey**.

---

# New Concept

## **"Tonight, You Don't Have To Be Strong."**

Instead of scrolling...

The website is one full-screen scene.

She clicks.

Things happen.

She explores.

It feels alive.

Think more like:

* Journey
* Monument Valley
* Before Your Eyes
* Florence
* Gris

rather than Apple's landing pages.

---

# Flow

She opens the site.

Only a night sky.

Stars.

Moon.

Tiny glowing particles.

Nothing else.

After 3 seconds...

One sentence fades in.

> **Hey.**

Pause.

> **I know today wasn't easy.**

Then

A tiny glowing lantern appears.

Text:

> **Pick it up.**

She clicks.

The lantern follows the cursor.

Immediately she realizes

"Oh..."

"I can interact."

Wherever she moves

Trees.

Flowers.

Stars.

Tiny animals.

Little details.

It literally symbolizes

"Even a little light changes everything."

## One idea I think would make her emotional in the best way

Since you're a developer, add one secret interaction.

In the photo section, let her click on **your favorite picture of her**.

Instead of just opening it, have a handwritten note appear:

> *"If someday you forget how wonderful you are, come back to this picture. Because this is how the world gets to see you—and I hope one day you can see yourself that way too."*

That message isn't about today's incident. It's about who she is, which gives it lasting value long after this difficult day has passed.
