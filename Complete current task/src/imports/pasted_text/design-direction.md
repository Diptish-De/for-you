I actually wouldn't make this as a "website" UI first.

I'd make it feel like an **interactive emotional experience**—almost like a short journey. Think of something between Apple, Studio Ghibli, Monument Valley, and a beautifully illustrated storybook.

The biggest mistake Figma AI will make is generating a generic landing page with cards, buttons, and sections. That's **not** what you want.

You want it to feel like **opening a tiny universe made just for one person.**

# The Prompt for Figma Make

> **Design an immersive single-page interactive emotional storytelling experience rather than a traditional website. The experience should feel like opening a beautifully illustrated digital storybook created for one person. Use cinematic hand-painted digital illustrations with atmospheric lighting, volumetric fog, glowing particles, painterly textures, and premium AAA concept-art quality inspired by Studio Ghibli environments, Journey, GRIS, Ori and the Blind Forest, Monument Valley, Firewatch, Child of Light, and Makoto Shinkai skies. Avoid anime, cartoon, or corporate UI aesthetics. Create five seamless full-screen scenes connected through elegant scrolling: (1) a moonlit bridge beneath a vast star-filled sky where a lone silhouetted girl quietly stands, creating an immediate sense of calm and safety; (2) a magical glowing forest with towering luminous trees, floating fireflies, warm lanterns, soft flowers, and a gentle fox, symbolizing a protected refuge; (3) a rain-soaked landscape where dark clouds and heavy rain represent emotional pain, while a glowing umbrella made of stars protects the girl, symbolizing resilience; (4) a tranquil lantern lake beneath a full moon where the user can release today's emotions into a floating lantern through a beautifully integrated journal interaction, with reflections, ripples, and hundreds of warm glowing lanterns filling the water; and (5) a peaceful mountain summit at sunrise with warm golden light breaking through the clouds, representing quiet hope rather than victory. End with a minimalist star-filled sky containing only a heartfelt closing message. Use a deep navy and midnight blue palette with soft lavender, warm cream, dusty pink, moonlight blue, subtle gold accents, and gentle glow effects. Typography should use elegant editorial serif headings paired with a modern sans-serif body font, featuring oversized headings, generous whitespace, and slow fade-in text animations. The interface should feel almost invisible, with floating glassmorphism buttons, subtle hover interactions, and no obvious cards, dashboards, or website sections. Every illustration should be highly detailed, emotionally expressive through the environment rather than facial expressions, with soft cinematic composition, environmental storytelling, painterly realism, premium lighting, and an atmosphere that communicates safety, warmth, and quiet companionship.**

## One suggestion that could make this unforgettable

At the end of the experience, don't sign it with **"From Diptish."**

Instead, have the final screen simply say:

> **Someone believes you'll get through this.**
>
> **Even if today makes that hard to believe.**

When she eventually realizes *you* built the entire experience, that realization will likely carry more emotional weight than seeing your name from the start. It lets the focus stay on her, rather than on the gesture itself.
