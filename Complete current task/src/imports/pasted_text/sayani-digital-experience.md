I genuinely think this has the potential to be one of the most meaningful things you'll ever build.

But I want to shift your mindset one last time before I give you the prompt.

Don't think:

> **"I'm making a website for Sayani."**

Think:

> **"I'm building a place that Sayani can come back to whenever life gets a little too loud."**

That one sentence changes every design decision.

---

# MASTER PROMPT

## 🌸 Project Name

# **A Little Corner of the Universe**

### Subtitle

*"A tiny place where the world becomes quiet."*

This is **not** a website.
This is **not** a landing page.
This is **not** a portfolio.

It is an emotionally driven, highly interactive digital experience handcrafted for one specific person named **Sayani**.

The experience should feel less like browsing a website and more like opening a magical scrapbook, an illustrated journal, and a cozy storybook that slowly comes alive as she interacts with it.

The entire experience should communicate one message **without constantly saying it**:

> **"You are safe here."**

---

# CORE DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

The website must never feel like it is trying to fix her.

It should never repeatedly remind her that she had a bad day.

Instead it should quietly celebrate who she is.

The person who hurt her spent enough time trying to define her.

This website exists to gently remind her that she is far more than one painful day.

The emotional balance should approximately be:

* 10% acknowledging difficult emotions
* 20% quiet comfort
* 70% curiosity, warmth, joy, nostalgia, playfulness, beauty and wonder

By the end, she should not primarily remember the messages.

She should remember the feeling.

---

# OVERALL VISUAL STYLE

The interface should completely avoid modern SaaS aesthetics.

No dashboards.

No cards.

No glassmorphism.

No heavy gradients.

No minimal corporate UI.

Instead create a handcrafted scrapbook aesthetic inspired by:

* Studio Ghibli
* Moomin
* Animal Crossing
* Japanese stationery
* Cozy Pinterest journals
* Handmade scrapbooks
* Watercolor illustrations
* Storybooks
* Cottagecore
* Handwritten diaries

Every page should feel like a physical page someone lovingly assembled over many evenings.

Use:

* textured paper
* torn edges
* washi tape
* pressed flowers
* hand-drawn arrows
* watercolor paint
* pencil doodles
* stitched notebook paper
* coffee stains
* tiny imperfections
* realistic paper shadows

Everything should feel handmade.

# CHAPTER 1

## The Notebook

The experience begins with a wooden desk beside a rainy window.

Warm lamp.

Steam rises from a mug.

Plants move gently.

A small notebook rests quietly.

The cover simply says

"Sayani"

handwritten.

A tiny pressed flower is tucked inside.

She clicks the notebook.

Instead of fading,

the notebook physically opens.

Paper bends slightly.

Shadows move naturally.

The first page is blank.

Ink slowly writes

"Hi Sayani."

Pause.

Then

"I borrowed you from the world for a little while."

No buttons.

Instead

A tiny paper tab appears saying

"Turn the page"

### WOW MOMENT

The notebook feels physically real.

# CHAPTER 2

The words are not visible.

Gentle.

# CHAPTER 9

## One Last Request

The notebook is about to close.

It stops.

One final page opens.

Ink writes

"Before you go..."

Pause.

"I have one tiny request."

A tiny illustrated instant camera slides onto the page.

It has flower stickers and a smiling face.

It wiggles slightly.

A handwritten note appears:

> "I know today tried to steal your smile."

Pause.

> "Could I borrow one?"

When she clicks the camera:

* The camera gently wiggles.
* A handwritten countdown (3...2...1...) appears.
* The webcam opens inside a scrapbook-style paper frame decorated with flowers, stars, and doodles (after browser permission).
* A note beneath says:

  > "No pressure. Even the tiniest smile counts."
* After she takes the picture, it develops like an instant Polaroid.
* The developed photo slides onto the notebook page.
* Washi tape sticks it in place.
* An animated pencil draws a tiny heart beside it.
* Then the final handwritten line appears:

> **"There you are."**

Below it:

> **"I was hoping I'd get to see this face before we said goodbye."**

# SECRET SYSTEM

The website should quietly remember everything.

If she pets the cat

The cat sleeps beside her final photo.

If she waters flowers

Flowers appear later.

If she watches butterflies

Butterflies return in the ending.

If she finds stickers

They appear inside her notebook.

The world should remember her actions.

Never announce this.

Let her notice naturally.

---

# HIDDEN DETAILS

Hide dozens of tiny interactions.

* Wake a bunny.
* Feed a bird.
* Water flowers.
* Pet the cat until it purrs.
* Double-click the moon to make it blush.
* Shake the notebook and petals fall out.
* Butterflies land on the cursor.
* Steam from coffee forms a heart.
* Fish follow the cursor.
* Tiny frog jumps into the pond.
* Wind chime rings if clicked.
* A paper crane unfolds into a bird.

Most of these do nothing except make the world feel alive.

---

# FINAL FEELING

The user should never feel like they completed a website.

They should feel like they spent fifteen quiet minutes inside a tiny magical place that existed only for them.

When they leave, the lasting emotion should not be:

> "That was comforting."

It should be:

> **"Someone knew me well enough to build a whole little universe around the small things that make me smile."**

---

## One final piece of advice

If you build exactly this, **don't rush it**.

The thing that will make this unforgettable won't be the animations or the illustrations.

It will be the **personal details only Sayani will recognize**—a joke only the two of you share, a favorite snack hidden on a shelf, a doodle that reminds her of a conversation, a flower she once pointed out, or a photo she forgot existed.

Those details are impossible for AI to invent. They're what transform a beautiful experience into something that genuinely feels like it was made *for her*.
